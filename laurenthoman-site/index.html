About

The work

Spatial experiences. Built in code.

I design and engineer for visionOS, Snap Spectacles, Unity, and Unreal. My foundation is in code — C#, C++, Python, Swift, JavaScript. What makes it distinct is a background in Maya and visual effects: texture, light, and interaction are never afterthoughts.

How I work

Engineer who designs. Designer who builds.

My major is Computer Science (Games) at USC Viterbi. I write the code for the experiences I design — from spatial UI prototypes to shipped builds. My coursework spans USC's School of Cinematic Arts and the Jimmy Iovine and André Young Academy. That training came to life at IndieCade, where I was lead designer, programmer, and artist on a 12+ person team.

Flow

The frequency of things

Flow is what happens when the craft takes over — and I find it in a lot of places. Music, DJing, the pool, a 3D scene that finally clicks. Spatial experiences are the hardest design challenge because they involve more senses than any screen ever will. When I put my grandparents in Bora Bora with a headset, or my mom pulled off an Apple immersive because the fear felt too real — those moments are the entire point.

Beyond the build

Community, travel, the spaces between

I'm Programming Chair at Art.Ificial — USC's first student org at the intersection of AI, entertainment, and creative technology. I travel constantly, make personal art to learn tools and express what code can't. I serve as VP of USC Women's Club Water Polo and run the team's social media.
